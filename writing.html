Ralph Tells a Story is a great book to teach students how to brainstorm a topic for their personal narrative writing.

Start by reading Ralph Tells A Story. As you read the story ask the following questions.

Questions

  • What problem does Ralph have?
  • How many of you have ever had the same problem as Ralph?
  • What does Ralph realize at the end of the story?
  • Can you think of any small moments you could write a story about?

After you read the story, model brainstorming 4 small moments using the graphic organizer. Last, have the students complete their own graphic organizer. They can work alone or with a partner.Once most students finish you can have a few share their work.

Knuffle Bunny is a great book to teach the characteristics of personal narrative writing.

Start by going over what characteristics a personal narrative should include.

A personal Narrative...

  • Focuses on a small moment.
  • Has a beginning, middle, end.
  • Explains how you or others felt.
  • Describes people, places, things. (Adjective, nouns)

Next, read Knuffle Bunny and have students listen for examples of each characteristic. After that, have the students complete the worksheet with a partner.
